# Sources & References: Rolling Calf

## Books

### Academic & Anthropological

- Jekyll, W. (1907). *Jamaican Song and Story: Annancy Stories, Digging Sings, Ring Tunes, and Dancing Tunes*. Folk-Lore Society, London.
  - First comprehensive collection of Jamaican folklore in English
  - Extensive Rolling Calf documentation including origin stories
  - Overseer and butcher transformation accounts
  - Foundation text for Jamaican folklore studies

- Beckwith, M.W. (1929). *Black Roadways: A Study of Jamaican Folk Life*. University of North Carolina Press.
  - Systematic anthropological fieldwork in Jamaica
  - Multiple Rolling Calf accounts from different parishes
  - Protective methods documented
  - Cultural context and African origins recognized
  - Comparative analysis with other Caribbean folklore

- Cassidy, F.G. & Le Page, R.B. (1967-1980). *Dictionary of Jamaican English* (2nd ed., 1980). Cambridge University Press. ISBN: 978-0521236553
  - Definitive documentation of Jamaican English including folklore terms
  - "Rolling Calf" entry with etymology and usage
  - Cultural context provided
  - Standard reference for Jamaican language studies

- Alleyne, M.C. (1988). *Roots of Jamaican Culture*. Pluto Press. ISBN: 978-0745302386
  - African cultural retentions in Jamaica
  - Rolling Calf as example of African continuity
  - Linguistic and cultural analysis
  - Theoretical framework for diaspora studies

- Barrett, L.E. (1976). *The Sun and the Drum: African Roots in Jamaican Folk Tradition*. Sangster's Book Stores, Kingston; Heinemann, London.
  - Comprehensive study of African influences on Jamaican folklore
  - Rolling Calf in context of African spiritual worldviews
  - Cattle symbolism and transformation beliefs
  - Religious syncretism analysis

- Leach, M. & Fried, J. (Eds.). (1949). *Funk & Wagnalls Standard Dictionary of Folklore, Mythology, and Legend*. Funk & Wagnalls.
